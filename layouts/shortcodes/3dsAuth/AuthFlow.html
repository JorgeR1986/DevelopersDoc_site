John buys a cellphone to be delivered in his house from his computer.

The cellphone shop, who is integrated to PayU authentication service, wants to verify that John is the one making the purchase, so they send this transaction to authenticate.

Through 3DS communication, the issuer receives the request to authenticate the transaction and start reviewing the shared data.

The issuer successfully authenticates the card used by John to perform the purchase. Given that the risk was low, the authentication was silent without contacting John.

When the transaction is risker for the issuer, John may receive a simple challenge, such as an OTP sent to his cellphone.

John receives the confirmation of his purchase and later on, he receives the cellphone in his house.
